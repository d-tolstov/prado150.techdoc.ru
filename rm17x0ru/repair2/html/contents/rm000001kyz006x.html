1. INSPECT V BELT



  1. Visually check the belt for cracks, oiliness or wear. Check that the belt does not touch the bottom of the pulley groove.
    Text in Illustration 
    *a
    		CORRECT
    *b
    		INCORRECT
    *c
    		Clearance
    If necessary, replace the belt.




  1. Check the vane pump V belt deflection by pressing on the belt at the point indicated in the illustration with 98 N (10 kgf, 22 lbf) of force.

    Standard Deflection:
    Item
    		Specified Condition
    New belt
    		8 to 10 mm
    (0.314 to 0.393 in.)
    Used belt
    		10 to 15 mm
    (0.393 to 0.590 in.)

    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Vane Pump Pulley
    *2
    		Crankshaft Pulley

    		Measuring Point for Belt Tension
    1. If necessary, adjust the vane pump V belt deflection.

  1. Reference:
    Using a belt tension gauge, check the cooler compressor V belt tension.

    Standard Tension:
    Item
    		Specified Condition
    New belt
    		441 to 539 N
    (45 to 55 kgf, 99.1 to 121.2 lbf)
    Used belt
    		196 to 343 N
    (20 to 35 kgf, 44.1 to 77.1 lbf)

    1. If the belt tension is not as specified, adjust it.

      ПРИМЕЧАНИЕ:
      1. Check the vane pump V belt tension at the specified point.
      2. When installing a new belt, set its deflection value as specified.
      3. Perform the V belt tension and deflection value check after idling the engine for a few minutes.
      4. When using a belt tension gauge, confirm the accuracy first by using a master gauge.
      5. When reinstalling a belt used for over 5 minutes, perform the check based on the used deflection value.
      6. When checking a belt used for over 5 minutes, confirm the deflection value is within the specified range.
